# Haskell Unfolder episode 55: Not quite monads

Monads are a very powerful abstraction: sometimes _too_ powerful. We discuss
why we might not always want the full generality of monads, and what we can
use instead. _Applicative_ functors are the most common alternative, but are
sometimes too restrictive; we highlight the fundamental difference between
applicative functors and monads, and finally introduce _selective_ functors,
which sit somewhere in between monads and applicative functors.
